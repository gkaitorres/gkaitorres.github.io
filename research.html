Thesis

How sovereign yields respond to GDP benchmarking events

My thesis examined how emerging-market sovereign yields respond to GDP benchmarking events, using an event-study framework to separate the effect of revised data from other macro news shaping prices.

Key finding

Yields declined around benchmarking events regardless of revision direction, suggesting the market was reacting to the attention and liquidity effects of the release itself.
