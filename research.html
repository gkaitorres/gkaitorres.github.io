Research & Work

Research focused on how markets absorb macro data, revisions, and the stories that follow.

My work is centered on the way economic data is interpreted in real time, especially when revisions can alter the narrative without changing the underlying signal in a straightforward way.

Focus areas

  • Event studies around macro releases and benchmark revisions
  • Fixed-income market responses to new information
  • Clear, readable analysis of economic signals and their limits

Thesis

How sovereign yields respond to GDP benchmarking events

My thesis examined how emerging-market sovereign yields respond to GDP benchmarking events, using an event-study framework to separate the effect of revised data from other macro news shaping prices.

Key finding

Yields declined around benchmarking events regardless of revision direction, suggesting the market was reacting to the attention and liquidity effects of the release itself.

Experience

Macro and data-focused projects

I’ve supported empirical work on macro and rates topics by cleaning data, building event-study code, and translating findings into concise notes for research discussions.

Those projects sharpened my interest in how market participants process new information under uncertainty and why clear framing matters.
