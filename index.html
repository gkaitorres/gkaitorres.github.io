About

I turn macro data, revisions, and market behavior into clear, evidence-based analysis.

I’m a recent University of Chicago Economics graduate with departmental honors, working toward research roles in macro and fixed income. I’m based in Chicago and open to relocating for the right opportunity.

My thesis focused on how sovereign yields respond to GDP benchmarking events, which pulled me toward the gap between how data revisions are reported and how markets actually price them. I’m especially interested in the moments when the story around a number matters as much as the number itself.

  • Writing on data revisions, market attention, and narrative framing
  • Exploring ways to connect economics, code, and clear presentation
